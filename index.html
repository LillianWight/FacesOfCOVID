The Pandemic is People and they are more than statistics

Peter Dario, Sr.

My dad died two days ago from COVID19. He died alone because the hospital restricted all visitors in order to contain the outbreak. My deepest regret was not saying bye to him when I brought him to the hospital. I thought it was going to be a “Take care, see you back home.”

Dr. Frank Gabrin

I lost my best friend to Covid19 a few hours ago. Dr Frank Gabrin was an ER Doctor in NYC. He planned to go back to work when he recovered. It took only five days from the first sign of symptoms. He leaves a husband of less than one year behind. We are devastated.

Natasha Ott, 39

A relative, Emily Coalson Stamets, called Ott “one of the smartest people I know.” Retired nurse Christiane Geisler, who worked with Ott, a social worker, at CrescentCare, said in an interview that she “never said 'no' to a client in need.”

Rebecca Mack, RN

She was an absolutely wonderful daughter. We couldn't have wished for a nicer daughter. She was so caring. She was just the light of our lives. I can't even begin to imagine life without her.

Belly Mujinga, 47

On 22nd March 2020, Belly and her colleague were on shift as ticket office staff ... at London’s Victoria Station. They were out on the concourse by the ticket office when they were assaulted by a member of the public who spat at them ... and told them he had the virus.... Within days of the assault, both women fell ill with the virus.... Belly died on 5 April, 14 days after she was assaulted at Victoria Station. The last time Belly’s husband saw her was when she was taken away in the ambulance.

Joe Mattern

Because this is too abstract for too many people: here’s my dad. The doctor phoned from a hospital a thousand miles away to say they’ve increased his morphine and he will succumb to the COVID-19 today, likely. He’s 76 and no one can be there to hold his hand.
